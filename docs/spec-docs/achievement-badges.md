# Achievement badges

### Purpose

Turn milestones into visible status. Reward meaningful actions across the full player journey.

### Product goals

* Celebrate progress outside raw collection count.
* Encourage varied play behavior.
* Give players shareable accomplishments.
* Mark memorable moments with lasting recognition.

### Player value

Badges give players proof of progress that feels more personal than levels.

They reward how a player plays, not only how much they play. This supports identity, pride, and feature discovery.

### Badge types

* First-time milestones.
* Rarity milestones.
* Collection milestones.
* Contextual milestones, such as event or time-based achievements.

### Why badges matter

Badges create meaning around standout moments. They help players remember what they did, not only what they earned.

They also encourage breadth. A player can care about scanning, collecting, exploring, social play, or event participation for different reasons.

### Example achievements

* First 10 cars.
* First Legendary.
* First car show capture.
* First night capture.

### Badge design principles

Badges should be easy to understand and satisfying to pursue.

* Each badge should represent a behavior or milestone worth celebrating.
* Names should feel memorable and human.
* Trigger logic should stay explainable.
* The badge list should mix quick wins with long-term goals.

### Design rules

* Every badge needs a clear trigger.
* Badge names should be memorable.
* Badges should range from easy to aspirational.
* Some badges should teach feature discovery.
* No badge should require confusing or hidden behavior.
* High-prestige badges should stay rare enough to matter.

### Badge tiers

Use tiers to organize emotional value.

#### Onboarding badges

These reward first actions. They teach the product and build early momentum.

#### Habit badges

These reward repeat behavior over time. They support retention and routine.

#### Mastery badges

These reward difficult, unusual, or high-commitment accomplishments. They create status.

#### Event badges

These mark time-bound participation or special moments. They work best when clearly separated from permanent badge tracks.

### Reward principles

* Every badge grants recognition.
* Select badges can also grant XP, coins, or cosmetics.
* Rewards should not force unhealthy grinding.
* Recognition should stay the primary value.
* Rewarding a badge should never make the trigger feel mandatory.

### Trigger guidance

Good badge triggers usually fall into a few patterns.

* First-time success.
* Accumulated progress.
* Behavioral variety.
* Social participation.
* Event involvement.

Avoid overly narrow triggers that players would never discover naturally.

### Progress visibility

Multi-step badges should show progress clearly. One-step badges should explain their trigger after unlock.

Players should be able to browse:

* Earned badges.
* Locked badges.
* In-progress badges.

This helps badges act as a motivation layer, not just a history log.

### UX principles

* Surface newly earned badges immediately.
* Let players browse locked and unlocked badges.
* Show progress toward multi-step badges.
* Distinguish badge recognition from economy rewards.
* Make prestige badges visually stronger without making the list noisy.

### Cross-system dependencies

Badges should reinforce surrounding systems.

* Progression can attach light XP or coin rewards.
* Rarity can power milestone badges.
* Sets can unlock collection-focused badges.
* Trading and leaderboards can support social recognition.
* Live ops can introduce temporary badge tracks.

### Guardrails

* Do not overwhelm players with too many early badges.
* Do not make badge requirements confusing.
* Do not tie important progression only to badges.
* Do not use badges to force repetitive grinding.
* Do not create so many badges that none feel memorable.

### Common failure risks

#### Badge spam

Too many low-value badges appear early. Recognition becomes noise.

#### Hidden logic

Players cannot tell why a badge triggered. Trust drops.

#### Reward distortion

Players chase badge rewards for efficiency, not meaning. Behavior becomes unnatural.

### Review questions

Use these questions when designing a badge.

* Is the trigger easy to explain in one sentence?
* Does this badge celebrate something players actually care about?
* Will the badge feel good even without extra currency?
* Does it encourage healthy play patterns?

### Success criteria

* Players understand why they earned a badge.
* Badges improve feature discovery and replay.
* Milestone moments feel worth sharing.
* Badge browsing creates future goals, not just history.
* High-value badges remain memorable over time.

### Out of scope

* Notification delivery systems.
* Technical event tracking.
