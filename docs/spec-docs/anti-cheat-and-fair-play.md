# Anti-cheat and fair play

### Purpose

Protect trust in collection progress. Keep the game fair without adding heavy friction.

### Product goals

* Preserve the value of rare finds.
* Stop obvious abuse.
* Explain fair play expectations clearly.

### Fair play principles

* Real-world captures should drive progress.
* Reused, manipulated, or fake submissions should not count.
* Enforcement should be proportional and understandable.

### Suspicious behavior categories

* Reused images.
* Internet-sourced uploads.
* Location manipulation.
* Artificial or simulated vehicles.
* Multi-account farming.

### Product responses

* Reject the submission.
* Reduce or remove rewards.
* Request another capture.
* Temporarily limit account actions.
* Escalate repeat abuse to suspension review.

### Player messaging

* Explain why a submission failed in simple language.
* Offer one next step when possible.
* Avoid accusatory wording on first failure.

### Enforcement guidelines

* Use low-friction responses for uncertain cases.
* Reserve stronger actions for repeated patterns.
* Protect honest players from false positives.

### Appeals and trust

* Players need a path to question penalties.
* Repeat trust signals should reduce future friction.
* Severe enforcement should include a clear reason.

### Success criteria

* Players view the system as fair.
* Abuse does not dominate rankings or rewards.
* Honest players can keep playing without confusion.

### Out of scope

* Detection models.
* Device and network implementation details.
