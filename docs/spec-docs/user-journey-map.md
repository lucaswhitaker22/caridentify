# User journey map

### Purpose

Map the full player experience. Show where the product must create clarity, delight, and return value.

### Product goals

* Improve first-session conversion.
* Identify key return moments.
* Align systems to the player lifecycle.

### Stage 1 — Discovery

#### Player mindset

The player is curious. They want to know if the concept works.

#### Product needs

* A clear value proposition.
* Immediate understanding of the main action.
* Low friction to first capture.

#### Success signal

The player starts a first scan quickly.

### Stage 2 — First success

#### Player mindset

The player wants proof. They need the first result to feel rewarding.

#### Product needs

* Fast identification result.
* Clear new-car celebration.
* Obvious reward feedback.

#### Success signal

The player completes the first collection moment.

### Stage 3 — Early habit formation

#### Player mindset

The player is testing whether the product has replay value.

#### Product needs

* Daily reasons to return.
* Early progression wins.
* Simple set or badge goals.

#### Success signal

The player returns within the next few days.

### Stage 4 — Mid-term engagement

#### Player mindset

The player wants goals beyond novelty. They begin to care about rarity and completion.

#### Product needs

* Collection depth.
* Visible long-term progress.
* Rarity-driven excitement.

#### Success signal

The player starts chasing specific targets.

### Stage 5 — Social and competitive play

#### Player mindset

The player wants recognition or collaboration.

#### Product needs

* Leaderboards.
* Trading.
* Shareable achievements.

#### Success signal

The player compares progress or trades with others.

### Stage 6 — Long-term retention

#### Player mindset

The player expects freshness. They stay only if the product keeps evolving.

#### Product needs

* Seasonal events.
* Ongoing rewards.
* New collection goals.

#### Success signal

The player returns across multiple weeks or seasons.

### Journey risks

* Confusion before the first capture.
* Weak reward feedback after the first success.
* No clear reason to return on day two.
* Long-term goals that feel unreachable.

### Design implications

* Optimize the first minute aggressively.
* Introduce goals in layers.
* Add social depth after the core loop proves itself.

### Success criteria

* Teams can map every major system to a journey stage.
* Drop-off points become easier to spot.
* Retention design follows player behavior, not assumptions.

### Out of scope

* Funnel analytics setup.
* Technical event instrumentation.
