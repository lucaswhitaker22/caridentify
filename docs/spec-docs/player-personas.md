# Player personas

### Purpose

Align the product around real player motivations. Guide feature priority, messaging, and progression design.

### Product goals

* Define who the product serves first.
* Clarify why players return.
* Prevent the product from serving every audience equally.

### Primary persona — The casual spotter

#### Profile

This player likes cars but is not deeply technical. They enjoy novelty, quick wins, and visible progress.

#### Motivations

* Spot something cool in the real world.
* Build a collection without heavy effort.
* Share interesting finds with friends.

#### Needs

* Fast capture flow.
* Clear rewards.
* Low learning curve.

#### Risks

* Leaves if the loop feels slow.
* Drops off if early rewards feel flat.

### Secondary persona — The collector

#### Profile

This player enjoys completion, rarity, and long-term goals. They want structure and reasons to keep playing.

#### Motivations

* Complete sets.
* Chase rare finds.
* Build status through progress.

#### Needs

* Deep garage browsing.
* Visible collection gaps.
* Meaningful completion rewards.

#### Risks

* Leaves if duplicates feel worthless.
* Frustrates if rarity feels random or unfair.

### Secondary persona — The social competitor

#### Profile

This player is driven by comparison and light rivalry. They want to see progress against friends.

#### Motivations

* Climb leaderboards.
* Trade strategically.
* Show off rare discoveries.

#### Needs

* Clear rankings.
* Fair trading rules.
* Social recognition.

#### Risks

* Leaves if rankings feel dominated by abuse.
* Disengages if social features arrive too late.

### Shared truths across personas

* The first session must feel effortless.
* Rare moments must feel memorable.
* Progress must remain visible in short sessions.

### Prioritization guidance

* Design the MVP for the casual spotter.
* Retain players through the collector loop.
* Expand engagement with social competition later.

### Success criteria

* Teams can explain the primary player in one sentence.
* Feature decisions map to at least one persona.
* Conflicting priorities become easier to resolve.

### Out of scope

* Demographic research methodology.
* Paid acquisition targeting.
